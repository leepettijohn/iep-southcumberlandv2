What residents share across the region

1.
Affordable housing leads everywhere. 44% of respondents across all five areas named it a top priority — the most consistent finding in the 2026 cycle.
2.
Workforce training is close behind. 43% named it a priority — driven heavily by parents and young adults across the region.
3.
Food insecurity is the metric people most want to move. 36% — more than one in three — named it as the indicator they hope to see improve in the next 1–3 years.
4.
More than half want to help. 95 of 182 respondents (52%) provided contact information indicating they want to volunteer or stay involved.

About this work

How the data was collected

Residents across the five South Cumberland communities completed a 49-question survey in early 2026. Questions covered economic perceptions, community confidence, priority programs and metrics, and basic demographics. Surveys were distributed through community foundations, libraries, schools, and churches.

Year-over-year comparisons draw from the matching 2025 cycle. Demographic context comes from U.S. Census ACS 5-year averages, the Tennessee Department of Education, and the Tennessee Department of Revenue.

How to use the reports

Each area page has three views. The Resident view is the public report — a letter from the foundation, what residents said in their own words, and the priorities the data points toward. The Director view is for foundation staff and contains analytical material; access is restricted. The Practitioner view is a methodology dossier for journalists, researchers, and program staff who want to dig into the numbers.
